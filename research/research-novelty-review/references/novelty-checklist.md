# Novelty Checklist

- What exactly is claimed to be new?
- Which part is the strongest contribution: framing, method, protocol, artifact, or result?
- Which prior works are closest, not just vaguely related?
- What is the single strongest overlap that could kill the novelty claim?
- Would a skeptical reviewer call this recombination, renaming, routine scaling, or a benchmark reshuffle?
- If the novelty is weak, should the recommendation be `ABANDON`, `REFRAME`, or `PROCEED`?
- Can the claim be narrowed to the smallest defensible contribution instead of the biggest marketable one?
