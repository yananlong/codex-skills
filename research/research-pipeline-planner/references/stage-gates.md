# Research Stage Gates

Use these gates to decide whether to proceed, revise scope, or stop.

## Literature review gate

- Proceed when the core question is specific enough to search.
- Revise when the topic is broad enough that search terms collapse into noise.
- Stop when the domain or research question is still missing.

## Novelty review gate

- Proceed when the proposed method or finding is concrete enough to compare.
- Revise when the idea is still rhetorical or bundles too many moving parts.
- Stop when search access or a corpus is unavailable.

## Experiment plan gate

- Proceed when the claim and decision rule are explicit.
- Revise when the plan still mixes multiple claims or confounded interventions.
- Stop when the evaluation target is undefined.

## Results audit gate

- Proceed when there is a real result artifact to inspect.
- Revise when the claimed conclusion is broader than the measured evidence.
- Stop when the headline numbers cannot be traced to source artifacts.

## Review loop gate

- Proceed when the artifact is important enough to benefit from iterative review.
- Revise when the artifact is too incomplete to support adversarial feedback.
- Stop when there is no concrete artifact to review.

## Paper plan gate

- Proceed when there is enough supported evidence to build a coherent paper story.
- Revise when claims still outrun the evidence.
- Stop when the user is asking for manuscript structure without any credible support.
