# Paper Review Rating Rubric

Use explicit 1-5 scales instead of `major / moderate / minor`.

## Issue impact rating

- `1`: cosmetic or extremely low decision relevance
- `2`: minor localized issue; worth fixing but unlikely to affect the paper-level decision
- `3`: meaningful localized issue; weakens a claim or section and needs a real fix
- `4`: major issue; materially threatens an important claim, comparison, or interpretation
- `5`: paper-level blocker; undermines a core methodological, evidential, or decision-critical claim

## Issue confidence rating

- `1`: weak hunch; plausible but poorly verified
- `2`: tentative; some supporting evidence but still uncertain
- `3`: moderate confidence; good support, but ambiguity remains
- `4`: strong confidence; supported by clear textual or cross-sectional evidence
- `5`: near-certain; directly verified and unlikely to be resolved by context

## Bundle summary ratings

- `overall_paper_rating`:
  - `1`: fundamentally unsound in current form
  - `2`: major structural weaknesses
  - `3`: mixed; publishability depends on fixes and venue standards
  - `4`: generally strong with notable but fixable issues
  - `5`: unusually strong; only limited concerns remain
- `decision_relevance_rating`:
  - `1`: findings are mostly polish-level
  - `3`: findings should influence a careful accept/revise decision
  - `5`: findings are clearly decision-critical
- `rating_confidence`:
  - same semantics as issue confidence, but for the bundle-level judgment
