# Commercialization Patterns

Use this file to avoid defaulting to "start a company" when a different path fits the asset better.

## 1) Spinout or startup

Best when the research enables a full product with repeatable customer value, a distinct wedge, and enough room to build a defensible company.

Watch for:

- long productization timelines disguised as immediate market readiness
- founder-market mismatch
- channel or regulatory complexity that overwhelms a new team

## 2) Outbound licensing

Best when the invention slots into an incumbent's roadmap, the primary asset is IP, and the research team is not the right operator for full commercialization.

Watch for:

- weak differentiation once implementation know-how is stripped away
- no natural buyer with both need and budget
- dependence on a single licensee's internal priorities

## 3) Joint development or strategic partnership

Best when domain access, distribution, manufacturing, regulatory capability, or installed workflow control matters more than building independently.

Watch for:

- becoming a custom R&D shop without durable leverage
- partnership incentives drifting away from the original thesis
- unclear ownership of resulting IP or customer relationships

## 4) Service-enabled product

Best when the technology is valuable but not yet turnkey, and service work can both de-risk the product and surface reusable patterns.

Watch for:

- services revenue masking the absence of a scalable product
- excessive customization
- teams getting trapped in delivery work without learning loops

## 5) Tooling, component, or infrastructure sale

Best when the research is an enabling layer used by other builders rather than a full end-user product.

Watch for:

- value capture leaking to downstream integrators
- underestimating support and integration burden
- weak differentiation once the interface is standardized

## 6) Data or evaluation asset

Best when the defensible value is a proprietary dataset, benchmark, model evaluation layer, or domain-specific insight engine rather than the raw algorithm alone.

Watch for:

- expensive data acquisition without pricing power
- poor refresh economics
- unclear ownership or rights over the underlying data

## 7) Hardware, instrument, or materials commercialization

Best when the research advantage is inseparable from physical performance, manufacturing know-how, or regulated deployment conditions.

Watch for:

- long validation and qualification cycles
- capital intensity hidden by early prototypes
- yield, supply-chain, and field-support risks
